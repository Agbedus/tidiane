Professional Biography
Science · Policy · Leadership · 30+ Years

Dr. Tidiane Ouattara is one of Africa's most distinguished space scientists and science diplomats. Born in Bobo-Dioulasso, Burkina Faso, he completed his undergraduate education in physics at the Université de Ouagadougou before earning his doctorate in astrophysics through a joint programme with the European Space Agency. His career encompasses frontier scientific research, multilateral diplomacy, institutional leadership, and public advocacy for science as a development tool.

President & Director General
African Space Agency (AfSA) — Cairo, Egypt
2021 — Present
Appointed by the African Union Assembly as inaugural head of the AfSA, the first continental body mandated to coordinate space policy and activities across all 54 AU member states. Established the agency's headquarters in Cairo; built operational divisions for Earth Observation, Navigation, Space Science, and Space Applications. Concluded partnership agreements with ESA, NASA, JAXA, ISRO, and CNSA. Chairs the Continental Space Council and represents Africa in all major international space governance forums.
Director of Space Programs
African Union Commission — Addis Ababa, Ethiopia
2015 — 2021
Led the six-year process to draft, consult on, and ratify the African Space Policy and Strategy. Established the African Space Coordination Committee; oversaw the design of the Africa Regional Integrated Multi-Hazard Early Warning System (RIMES) satellite component. Coordinated with 32 member states to harmonise Earth observation data sharing legislation.
Senior Scientist — Remote Sensing & EO
ISRO-Africa Collaborative Program · New Delhi / Dakar
2008 — 2015
Led multi-spectral analysis research for the Sahel Earth Observation Initiative. Developed algorithms for land-cover change detection applied to desertification and food security monitoring. Published eleven peer-reviewed papers in journals including Remote Sensing of Environment and IEEE Geoscience. Supervised eight doctoral candidates.
Research Fellow — Astrophysics
Université de Ouagadougou / ESA Fellowship · France & Burkina Faso
1999 — 2008
Doctoral thesis on low-luminosity galaxy stellar populations; post-doctoral fellowship at ESA's ESTEC (Noordwijk). Recipient of the ESA Young Researcher Award (2003). Early-career advocate for the establishment of radio telescope infrastructure across West Africa.
