About

Architect of Africa's
Space Sovereignty

Dr. Tidiane Ouattara has dedicated over three decades to advancing space science, satellite communications, and Earth observation across the African continent. As the inaugural President of the African Space Agency, he leads a continental mandate to coordinate, harmonize, and amplify Africa's capacities in space.

His work bridges science and diplomacy — forging partnerships with ESA, NASA, JAXA, and CNSA while ensuring that space technology translates directly into agricultural resilience, disaster preparedness, and climate action for African communities.

"Space is not a luxury for Africa. It is infrastructure — as essential as roads, as vital as water."

Career Highlights

A Lifetime of
Scientific Leadership

2021—
President & Director General
African Space Agency (AfSA), Cairo, Egypt
Appointed as the inaugural head of the newly established continental agency, mandated under the African Union to coordinate all member-state space programs and represent Africa in global space governance.
2015–21
Director of Space Programs
African Union Commission, Addis Ababa
Led the drafting of the African Space Policy and Strategy; coordinated Earth observation data-sharing frameworks across 32 member states.
2008–15
Senior Scientist — Remote Sensing
ISRO-Africa Collaborative Program / New Delhi & Dakar
Conducted pioneering research in multi-spectral analysis for land degradation monitoring across the Sahel and Horn of Africa regions.
1999–08
Research Fellow — Astrophysics
Université de Ouagadougou / ESA Fellowship Program
Doctoral and post-doctoral work on stellar classification in low-luminosity galactic environments; recipient of the ESA Young Researcher Award (2003).
